Steak with Pan Sauce

Entree

Prep 60 mins Makes 4 Difficulty Medium Source Skillet Recipes

Ingredients

  • 2 beef top loin or ribeye steaks, cut 1 to 1.5" thick
  • OR
  • 4 beef tenderloin steaks, cut 1 to 1.5" thick
  • salt
  • black pepper
  • 6 tbsp cold unsalted butter
  • 1/2 C dry red wine OR apple juice
  • 1/3 C reduced-sodium beef broth
  • 3 tbsp shallot OR 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 tbsp whipping cream

Directions

# Preparation

1. Allow steaks to stand at room temperature for 30 minutes

2. Preheat oven to 400 degrees

3. Finely chop shallot OR Mince garlic

4. Trim fat from steaks

5. Pat steaks dry with a paper towel

# Directions

1. Sprinkle salt and pepper over all sides of steaks. Set aside.

2. Heat a large oven-ready skillet over medium-high heat.

3. Add 2 tbsp butter to hot skillet, then reduce heat to medium.

4. Add steaks to skillet. Cook about 2 minutes each side or until browned. Transfer skillet to oven.

5. Roast, uncovered, for 10-13 mins or until medium-rare (instant-read thermometer measures 145 degrees when inserted in center).

6. Transfer steaks to a serving platter and cover with foil.

7. Drain fat from skillet, then add wine / juice, broth, and shallot / cloves to skillet.

8. Bring to boiling, scraping up any crusty browned bits from bottom of skillet.

9. Boil gently, uncovered, over medium heat about 6 minutes or until liquid is reduced to about 1/4 C.

10. Whisk in whipping cream. Boil gently for 1-2 mins more or until slightly thickened.

11. Whisk in remaining 4 tbsp butter, 1 tbsp at a time, whisking until butter is melted and sauce is thickened.

12. Serve steaks with pan sauce.
